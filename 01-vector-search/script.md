## SCRIPT

---

When you search a traditional database, you're doing keyword matching. You ask for documents that contain the word "fast," and you get back documents that contain exactly that word. Nothing more.

But what if you want to find documents about "high-performance computing" — or "low-latency systems" — or "quick processing pipelines"? Those concepts are related. They mean similar things. But keyword search will miss them entirely.

That's where **semantic search** comes in.

---

Semantic search works on *meaning*, not exact words. The key ingredient is something called an **embedding** — a mathematical representation of your data as a list of numbers, or a vector. Words, sentences, even entire documents can be converted into these high-dimensional vectors, where position in space reflects conceptual meaning.

If two pieces of text mean similar things, their vectors will be *close* to each other in this vector space. If they mean different things, they'll be far apart. Searching becomes a question of finding your nearest neighbors in that space — rather than matching character strings.

---

Now here's where **RavenDB** comes in — and this is where it gets interesting.

Starting with RavenDB 7.0, vector search is built directly into the database. You don't need a separate vector store. You don't need to manage a parallel infrastructure. It's all integrated.

Under the hood, RavenDB uses its **Corax** index engine to power vector fields. When you define a vector field in your index, RavenDB builds an **HNSW graph** — Hierarchical Navigable Small World — a state-of-the-art algorithm for Approximate Nearest Neighbor search that scales efficiently to millions of vectors in high-dimensional space.

---

There are two ways to run vector search in RavenDB.

The first is a **dynamic query**. You write a query against a collection, and RavenDB automatically creates a Corax auto-index behind the scenes. No index definition required — great for exploration and prototyping.

The second approach is a **static index**, where you explicitly define a vector field using either `CreateVector()` — for raw text or numerical arrays — or `LoadVector()` — for embeddings pre-generated by RavenDB's Embedding Generation Tasks.

Those tasks integrate directly with providers like **OpenAI**, **Azure OpenAI**, **Hugging Face**, **Google AI**, **Ollama**, and **Mistral AI**. Or, if you prefer, RavenDB ships with a built-in embedding model — **bge-micro-v2** — so you can generate embeddings with zero external dependencies.

---

At query time, you use RavenDB Query Language — RQL — with the `moreLikeThis` or vector similarity operators to find the most semantically similar documents. You can tune the minimum similarity threshold, control the number of candidate neighbors evaluated during search, and even combine vector fields with standard full-text, spatial, or structured fields — all in a single index.

---

The result is a system where your operational database and your AI-powered semantic search layer are the same thing. No data duplication. No sync jobs. No extra infrastructure to manage.

Whether you're building a semantic search engine, a recommendation system, or a Retrieval-Augmented Generation pipeline for an LLM, RavenDB gives you the vector capabilities you need — right where your data already lives.
